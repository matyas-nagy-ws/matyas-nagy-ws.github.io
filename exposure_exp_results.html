Experiment: direct-debit-t3-insuff-exposure

Slowing down high-exposure ACH payments instead of blocking them. Did allowing more payments through increase losses?

Period: Mar 28 – Aug 18, 2025
Control (V0): 67,839 users
Treatment (V1): 67,740 users
Status: Archived
How this experiment worked: When a customer's outstanding exposure (money Wise hasn't yet received from the bank) exceeded a threshold, the Control group had payments blocked entirely via usability preconditions. The Treatment group instead had payments slowed down to NSF_SAFE speed (wait for bank settlement before sending payout), allowing the transfer to proceed while mitigating risk.

Key takeaway: The treatment successfully unlocked +$43.6M in payment volume while keeping net losses nearly flat (+$27K / +1.9%). The loss rate per dollar of volume actually decreased by 1.6%, driven by a 3.3pp improvement in recovery rate on slowed payments.

Experiment Mechanism: Block vs Slow

Monthly count of payment attempts blocked (ACCOUNT_EXPOSURE) vs slowed (EXPOSURE_SLOW_PAYMENT) by variant

In the Control, ~199K payment attempts were blocked at the precondition stage. In the Treatment, only ~5K were blocked — the rest were allowed through as slowed payments. This resulted in 26,890 more completed transfers and $43.6M more volume.

⚠️
The NSF_SAFE share nearly doubled (8.6% → 15.7%). This means more customers experienced slower payments. However, these customers would have been completely blocked in the control — a slower payment is better than no payment.
Chargebacks increased by +10.4% in absolute count, but when normalized by payment volume the rate only increased by +7.6%. Most of the increase came from R01 (Insufficient Funds) and 901 return codes, which is expected since these are the payments that were previously blocked.

💡
Critical insight: The NSF_SAFE chargeback rate in the treatment (213.5/10K) is 42% lower than in the control (369/10K). In the control, only the highest-risk payments are NSF_SAFE. In the treatment, the pool is diluted with moderate-risk payments (formerly blocked), bringing down the average rate.
The bottom line: despite more chargebacks, net losses after recovery increased by only +$27K (+1.9%). The recovery rate improved from 67.3% to 70.6% because NSF_SAFE payments settle before payout, making it easier to recover funds when a chargeback occurs.
