About ACH Debits

An ACH debit is an electronic payment where funds are "pulled" from an account. Common examples include utility bills, subscription services, and mortgage payments.


Key Participants:

  • Originator: The entity initiating the debit (e.g., a business)
  • ODFI (Originating Depository Financial Institution): The originator's bank
  • ACH Operator: The central clearing facility (Federal Reserve or The Clearing House)
  • RDFI (Receiving Depository Financial Institution): The receiver's bank
  • Receiver: The account holder whose account is being debited

Typical Timeline:

  • Day 1: Originator submits debit request to ODFI
  • Day 2: ODFI forwards to ACH Operator, which sends to RDFI
  • Day 3: Settlement occurs, funds are transferred
  • Day 4+: Potential returns (e.g., NSF) can occur within 2 business days
